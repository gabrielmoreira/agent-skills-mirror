Sources and best practices that informed the create-briefing skill templates.

## General Business Documents

- [Business Documents: Types, Formatting, and Best Practices](https://www.upcounsel.com/business-document-format) - Overview of common business document types
- [Visme: Business Documents Guide](https://visme.co/blog/business-documents/) - 30+ common business document examples

### Key Insight

> "Most leaders skim. They look for what the document is about, what you want from them, and what the following action is. If they must hunt through paragraphs and images, they stop reading and postpone the decision."

## Amazon 6-Pager / One-Pager

- [Six Pager Memo](https://www.sixpagermemo.com/blog/amazon-six-pager-template) - Amazon Six Pager Template
- [Visme: Amazon 6-Pager Guide](https://visme.co/blog/amazon-6-pager/) - Guide, templates & tips
- [Writing Cooperative: Anatomy of an Amazon 6-pager](https://writingcooperative.com/the-anatomy-of-an-amazon-6-pager-fc79f31a41c9) - Deep dive
- [Lark Suite: Amazon 6-Pager](https://www.larksuite.com/en_us/blog/amazon-6-pager) - What, Why, and How (2025)

### Standard Structure

1. Introduction
2. Goals
3. Tenets
4. State of the Business
5. Lessons Learned
6. Strategic Priorities

### Key Rules

- No bullet points, no graphics, no fluff
- Full sentences and paragraphs that flow naturally
- Appendix for supporting graphs, images, tables (no page limit)
- Read in silence for 30 minutes at start of meetings

## Decision Documents (DACI)

- [Atlassian: DACI Framework](https://www.atlassian.com/team-playbook/plays/daci) - Decision-making framework
- [Project Management: DACI Model](https://project-management.com/daci-model/) - Everything you need to know
- [LogRocket: DACI Framework Examples](https://blog.logrocket.com/product-management/daci-framework-examples-template/) - Examples and template

### DACI Roles

- **D = Driver**: Corrals stakeholders, collates info, drives to decision
- **A = Approver**: The ONE person who makes the final decision
- **C = Contributors**: Subject-area knowledge, voice but not vote
- **I = Informed**: Need to know about progress

### When to Use

- Complex decisions with multiple stakeholders
- High-stakes organizational decisions
- Cross-functional projects

## Status Updates

- [Publicate: Weekly Update Email Templates](https://publicate.it/blog/weekly-update-email) - Best practices
- [Asana: Project Status Reports](https://asana.com/resources/how-project-status-reports) - 8-step guide with template
- [Status.net: 20 Weekly Update Templates](https://status.net/articles/weekly-update-email-templates-examples/) - Examples

### Best Practices

- Send at predictable times (Monday morning or Friday afternoon)
- Use consistent format week to week
- Short, digestible sections with large headings
- Include: progress, blockers, next steps, decisions needed

## Post-Mortems / Retrospectives

- [Atlassian: Incident Postmortem Templates](https://www.atlassian.com/incident-management/postmortem/templates) - Template collection
- [FireHydrant: Incident Retrospective Template](https://firehydrant.com/blog/incident-retrospective-postmortem-template/) - Ultimate template
- [incident.io: Post-Mortem Template](https://incident.io/hubs/post-mortem/incident-post-mortem-template) - Simple template

### Key Principle

> "A truly effective post-mortem focuses on what went wrong, not who went wrong. When teams embrace a blameless approach, post-mortems become powerful tools for growth."

### Common Sections

- Incident summary (duration, causes, effects)
- Timeline of events
- Root cause analysis ("5 Whys")
- Impact assessment
- Lessons learned
- Action items with owners and deadlines

### What to Include

- Overview describing what report/proposal will entail
- Problem you're solving
- Steps/methods to solve it
- Keep each section to no more than 5 sentences

## Dual-Audience Pattern

The "human-readable top, AI context bottom" pattern is emerging as AI agents increasingly consume documents. Key principles:

1. **Human section**: Scannable, conclusions first, clear ask
2. **AI section**: Structured data, raw sources, definitions, reasoning chain

This allows documents to serve both immediate human readers and future AI agents who need to understand context.
